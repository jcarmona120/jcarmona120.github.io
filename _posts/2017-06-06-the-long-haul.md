### What I Learned Today

** A Quick One to remember about the DOM **

You can call the querySelector() or querySelectorAll() method on more than just the document. You can call it on the elements within that assigned variable.

```javascript
const burger = document.querySelector('div');
const toppings = burger.querySelectorAll('div > p')
```

So I was able to declare the burger as the div and then use toppings to find all the 'p' child elements in that div.
